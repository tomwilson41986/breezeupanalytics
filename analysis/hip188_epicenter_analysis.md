### Positives

1. **Elite raw time (9.4s)** — Ties for fastest 1/8-mile time in the entire sale (ranked 2nd). Top 1-2% on stopwatch.
2. **Clean breeze conditions** — 0 mph wind, 62°F, Fair track. No tailwind inflation — legitimate bullet work.
3. **Epicenter x More Than Ready cross** — Sire was a top-class racehorse (2nd KY Derby, 2nd Preakness). More Than Ready as broodmare sire adds stamina and proven class.
4. **Value entry point** — RNA'd at KEESEP for $35K. Potential for good risk/reward if sale price stays moderate.
5. **Both videos available** — Breeze and walk videos for full physical assessment.
6. **Solid gallop-out through the quarter** — 20.1 (1/4), 32.3 (3/8), 46.4 (1/2) shows she carried speed. Adjusted 3/8 of 37.03 is competitive.

### Negatives / Concerns

1. **Below-average stride length** — 22.83 ft (UT) ranked 67th; 21.65 ft (GO) ranked 97th. Running fast via high frequency (2.63) rather than ground-covering strides. "Busy" running style may not sustain at distance.
2. **Stride shortening in gallop-out (-0.36)** — Ranked 95th. Significant drop suggests maximum effort during breeze with quick fatigue, or lack of natural length to carry speed.
3. **Composite rating only 54.7** — Middle of the pack when stride quality is factored in. The gap between time rank (2nd) and composite (54.7) is one of the widest in the sale.
4. **Unproven first-crop sire** — Zero data on whether Epicenter progeny can run. Stud fee dropped from $45K→$25K, reflecting market caution.
5. **RNA at $35K yearling** — Market saw something it didn't love as a yearling.
6. **Filly residual risk** — Dam (More Than Folly) not a proven producer. Needs to run to justify price.
7. **Weakest stride profile among Epicenter siblings** — Hip 63 (Epicenter filly) rated 87.3 with a 10.1 time and far better stride mechanics. Hip 188 is fastest by clock but worst on stride quality in the Epicenter group.
